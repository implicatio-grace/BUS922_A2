BUS922 A2 · The slice

The Learn slice — one person's act, end to end

The one focused part this study designs: a person takes a real problem from their own work and talks or writes it through; a purpose-built template (with AI) does the organising, so the person just explains; what's left is a rich, well-structured record ready for others to reuse. One act does two jobs at once — the person learns, and the business gains a reusable record. That double payoff is the idea the study turns on.

Raw material · from the partner's people The hands-on know-how the people in each acquired business carry, across 200+ offices — client relationships, local regulations, market conditions — a real asset that can stay hidden without a simple way to surface it.
↓
DP1 · raw material

Authentic operational problem

Start from a real problem the person brings or picks — not a made-up exercise. It is realism, not difficulty, that makes learning stick and carry over to new situations. How adults learn best (andragogy) guides the design here.

For the partnerA real freight, customs or client case from the person's own office.
Herrington et al. 2013 · Perusso & Baaken 2020 · Knowles 1975
→
DP2 · generation

Generative articulation

The person writes (or records) a short, prompted account of the problem. Explaining it in their own words is what drives the learning, and the act of explaining is both the learning and the record itself.

For the partnerCaptures the know-how people carry but rarely write down.
Bisra et al. 2018 · Fiorella & Mayer 2016 · Schön 1987
→
DP3 · the template HEADLINE

Structuring lives in the template

A purpose-built AI template carries the structure — it prompts reflection and adds the titles, tags and labels automatically. The person only has to explain; the template does the organising, and eases off gently as they gain experience.

For the partnerPutting the organising work into the template is what makes this workable in a firm with no dedicated professional-development team.
van Merriënboer & Boot 2005 · & Sweller 2010 · Tetzlaff et al. 2025 · Salden et al. 2010
→
DP4 · the output

Context-rich intermediate

Capture a rich, in-context record and stop there — the artefact this slice produces (the context-rich intermediate of the learning-design literature, and called simply the record in the plainer text throughout: one output, three names for the same thing). Getting it ready for wider reuse comes later — that's the Share stage's job. Don't strip out the context when it's created; the context is what makes it valuable.

For the partnerThis is the join where one person's know-how becomes shared knowledge the whole business can use.
Markus 2001 · Rivière et al. 2019 · Szulanski 2000 · Kasper et al. 2010

The double payoff · learning and a record at once
generate = learn + record
One act, done once — the person is more capable, and the business gains a reusable record. Not two tasks; the same task.
What the act leaves behind

A shareable record

Rich in context, structured by the template, ready for the next person and the wider network — without asking the author to do the organising.

Out of scope · post-A3 · landscape-informing DP5 and DP6 sit just beyond this study's focused part. The full detail lives here so it stays put — the evidence landscape maps the supporting research, which grows over time. These inform the direction rather than being part of the formal design.
DP5 · post-A3 · landscape-informing

Learner-controlled disclosure as a trust mechanism

Reflect privately first, then choose what to share — the person controls what becomes visible. Sharing is treated as both a safe and a productive choice, not an automatic post.

For the partnerFeeling safe to contribute is how newly-acquired staff from different backgrounds start to belong — a point from the partner's culture brief.

Why deferredTrust and sharing open up their own body of evidence. The formal design stops at capture, before the decision to share — so DP5 guides the direction without widening the focused part.

Connelly et al. 2011 · Petronio 2010 · Frazier et al. 2017

DP6 · post-A3 · landscape-informing

Multiple means of expression, no single channel privileged

Let people explain in whatever form suits them best — writing, speaking, diagrams. Universal Design for Learning (UDL) is the fairness lens; the learning benefit is the payoff.

For the partnerLets the most hard-to-put-in-words know-how surface in non-written form, where plain text would lose it.

Why deferredMultiple formats multiply the design work for the record and the template. The study deliberately fixes one format to isolate how the explaining works (DP2–DP3); DP6 shows where it widens later.

King-Sears et al. 2023 · Epstein et al. 2020 · Zhang et al. 2024 · Capp 2017
